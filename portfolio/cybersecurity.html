Cybersecurity Challenges

2018: Designed and developed a single-page fake "phone" with configurable social apps for use in teaching cyberscurity, while working as a freelance web developer.

Description

The challenge: The Cybersecurity Challenges are mock wargames to teach students about cybersecurity. Fake versions of popular social media sites are populated with fake profiles and posts. Students are asked to find personal user information, and learn about cybersecurity and privacy from the point of view of the "hacker".

My role: I designed the API by which the content of the social media apps could be configured by problem authors. I also "branded" and design of the fake apps, and built it as a single-page app (including the configurable apps in the mobile on the left, and problem on the right). The microsite also needed to record and emit certain user events for marking purposes.
